Sampling distributions

In science, to make a discovery you need to know your error. Error is the god to which we all need to pray. One of the best ways to assign error to measurements is using Bayesian inference. Bayesian inference assumes that you can never truly know anything, instead you can consolidate evidence (i.e. data) to may decisions and inference about parameters or models that generate the data. To do this first lets define our model MMM with parameters θ\thetaθ. Furthermore, we will assume that our data yyy is generated from some noisy process.
